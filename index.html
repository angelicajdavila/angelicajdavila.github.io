Angelica Julia Davila

Who dat?

Angelica is the child of immigrants, which makes her a speaker of two languages, and master of none. Similar to her bilingual abilities, she also likes to do a variety of things. Having lost Fanta’s “4th Fantana Search” back in 2010, Angelica has chosen to spend her time pursuing comedy and writing. It is, however, said that she can often be heard whispering “Want a Fanta, don’t you wanta” during her sleep. When Angelica isn’t busy writing or telling jokes, she works at her big, girl job as an autism and developmental delays consultant for parents, teachers, and other professionals because her family always told her to have a back up career.

The Writer

She went to school for this!

Angelica is currently working toward obtaining her Master’s in Creative Writing at University of Illinois at Chicago. She also has a a Bachelor of Arts in Creative Writing and Advertising from DePaul University.

Hear her stories, LIVE!

Angelica is a regular at Bad Grammar Theater, a reading series featuring established and rising Chicago authors sharing the best in horror, fantasy, pulp fiction, science fiction, and the unexpected.

She won something, that one time!

Angelica received the 2010 Kate Herzog Award from Willamette Writers for her short story, “Tips”.

Like comics? She does, too!

Angelica was the writer of a writer and illustrator duo for Piggybits. While Piggybits is currently on hold, you can still check out a few samples from the good old days.
